20250209-CAS1: Casualty Data Overview (2023–2024)

Casualty Data Overview

Source: Hamas-run Gaza Ministry of Health, Israeli official reports

Gaza:

  • Total killed: 41,000–45,000
  • Children: 13,667–14,000 (31.6%–44%)
  • Women: 4,959–8,241 (20.1%–20.5%)
  • Displaced: 1.9M (85% of population)

Israel:

  • Total killed: 1,200–1,706
  • Civilians: 800–860
  • IDF soldiers: 329–346

Hostages: 250 taken, 112 released, 97 remaining, 33 deceased [1].

20250209-ETH1: Ethical Challenges in Reporting

Ethical Challenges in Reporting

Data Limitations and Politicization

  • Gaza’s data is sourced from Hamas-controlled sources with limited independent verification.
  • Estimates include 10,000+ buried under rubble (Euro-Med Monitor).
  • Discrepancies: IDF claims 14,000 Hamas fighters killed versus Hamas denial.

Key Insight: Underreporting and politicization create "dark matter" in conflict statistics.

20250209-VIS1: Cinematic Storytelling Techniques

Cinematic Storytelling Techniques

Camera Work and Transitions

  • Camera Work: Use of close-ups (e.g., Leica M3 for intimate portraits such as Yasmin’s doll).
  • Lighting: Diffused moonlight (Hasselblad) and harsh shadows (Nikon F3) create contrasting moods.
  • Transitions: Techniques such as the blink effect (fade-to-smoke) and digital pixel glitches create surreal transitions between scenes.

Example: A "Hypnotic Dream Sequence" that loops between an IDF soldier and a Gaza child.

20250209-SOC1: Displacement and Resource Crisis

Displacement and Resource Crisis

Structural Impact on Gaza

  • Gaza: 1.9 million displaced; 60% of shelters damaged (UNRWA data).
  • Resource Strain: Only 10% of hospitals functional (WHO), 2L water per person per day (WHO minimum is 15L).
  • Urban infrastructure: 45% destroyed (2024 satellite imagery).

20250209-POL1: Political Narratives and Impact

Political Narratives and Impact

Legitimacy and Rhetoric in Conflict

  • Hamas: Uses casualty statistics from its own health ministry for legitimacy.
  • Israel: Emphasizes Hamas combatant ratios (claims of 14,000 fighters killed) to justify military actions.
  • International Law: Debates over proportionality and interpretations of military necessity.

Paradox: A high civilian toll contrasted with arguments of military necessity.
