Project Seed: Redefining AI for Human-Centered Empowerment

AI-001: Overview & Vision

Reclaiming Human Agency in the AI Era

This project seed interrogates the paradoxes of AI disclosure and the unintended consequences of AI integration in education, work, and organizational design. It critiques how excessive training and opaque usage justify roles and enforce compliance instead of fostering genuine human creativity, critical inquiry, and agency.

Vision: Redefine mastery by empowering users to use AI as a collaborative partner—not as a replacement for foundational skills. The goal is to create systems that are transparent, encourage exploration, and maintain human control over AI processes.

AI-002: Key Themes

Disclosure, Impact, and Design Principles

  • AI Disclosure Paradox: Tension between hidden usage (competitive secrecy) and the demand for full transparency. Contradictions include competitive advantage vs. ethical accountability and copyright preservation vs. technical openness.
  • Educational and Organizational Impact: AI tools risk producing compliant workers and passive learners by bypassing foundational skills, reducing agency and critical thinking.
  • Design Principles for Generative AI:
    • Design for Exploration – embrace generative variability.
    • Design for Human Control – include adjustable parameters and clear feedback.
    • Design for Mental Models – provide transparent explanations.
    • Design for Imperfection – build in iterative improvement and error correction.
  • Ethical and Critical Frameworks: Ground the analysis in postphenomenology and STS to redefine work and learning in the AI era through interdisciplinary dialogue.

Workshops and Call to Action

  • Workshop: "AI Regulation: A Postphenomenological Mapping of the Challenges" on Friday, October 25th, 2:30–4:00 pm at Savant Conference Room 115.
  • Discussion Focus: Reframe AI from an enforcement tool to a means of amplifying human potential. Integrate foundational coding and critical thinking with AI for a more inclusive, human-centered future.

AI-004: Underlying Concerns

Latent Discontents in AI Integration

  • Students and workers feel disempowered by AI’s "black box" nature.
  • Reliance on AI shortcuts creates a cognitive disconnect from foundational skills.
  • The shift to AI-assisted creation lacks proper scaffolding, leading to lost agency and superficial learning.
