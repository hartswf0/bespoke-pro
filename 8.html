Early AI & Language Models

20240209-AF1: Early NLP and Symbolic AI

Terry Winograd's SHRDLU (1972)

Key Insight: SHRDLU demonstrated natural language understanding in a constrained "blocks world" using parsers and semantic rules, laying the foundation for task-oriented chatbots.

Equation: Understanding = Syntax + Semantics + Context

→ Links: AF3 (Language Models vs. Innate Grammar), LEG2 (Code as Regulation)

20240209-AF2: Agent-Based Modeling (ABM)

Simulating Emergent Complexity

Key Insight: Decentralized agent interactions model complex phenomena, from market dynamics to protest movements, exemplifying Ashby’s Law of Requisite Variety.

Case Study: Pandemic spread simulations with 86% accuracy in 2020.

→ Links: CS1 (Cybernetic Socialism), MIS2 (Five Stages of AI Grief)

20240209-AF3: Language Models vs. Innate Grammar

Piantadosi’s Challenge to Chomsky

Key Argument: Modern LLMs (e.g., GPT-4) achieve fluency without innate grammatical structures, relying on statistical predictions from massive datasets rather than hard-wired generative rules.

Data: LAION-5B trains models on next-token prediction rather than traditional parsing.

→ Links: LEG1 (Informational Capitalism), MIS1 (The Bitter Lesson)

Legal and Regulatory Frameworks

20240209-LEG1: Informational Capitalism

Julie Cohen’s Legal Critique

Key Thesis: Law constructs data as a commodity, enabling monopolistic practices such as Meta’s targeted advertising.

Case Example: Cambridge Analytica's use of Facebook data to influence voter behavior.

→ Links: AF3 (Language Models), CS4 (Algorithmic Persuasion)

20240209-LEG2: Code as Regulation

Lessig’s "Pathetic Dot" Framework

Key Model: Law, norms, market, and code collectively regulate behavior. Algorithmic bias often emerges from the architecture (the "dots") in code, not merely from legal gaps.

→ Links: LEG1 (Informational Capitalism), CS4 (Algorithmic Persuasion)

20240209-LEG3: Law of the Horse Revisited

AI’s Regulatory Singularity

Key Argument: AI’s cross-domain impact necessitates tailored laws rather than outdated analog-era models, as exemplified by the EU AI Act rejecting "horse" analogies.

→ Links: LEG1 (Informational Capitalism), LEG2 (Code as Regulation)
