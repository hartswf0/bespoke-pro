Why Software Is Eating the World

Overview

Software’s Transformation of Society

Mobile platforms (think Zynga, Angry Birds) aren’t just delivering software—they’re redefining it. Like the McDonald’s drive‐thru of tech, these platforms enforce speed, scale, and standardization that transform bespoke digital experiences into a rapid, commoditized metabolism.

This “digital drive‐thru” creates new pathologies: attention fragmentation, echo chambers, and a commodification of outrage that trades quality engagement for sheer volume.

AI and Software Industrialization

From Master Chefs to McCode™

Software engineering is undergoing an industrial revolution. Once the domain of master chefs crafting unique experiences, the rise of AI—what some call McCode™—has shifted production from scarce human talent to an overabundance of automated code. With forecasts like Zuckerberg’s 2025 vision of AI replacing mid-level engineers, innovation is now driven by access to powerful computational resources, hinting at a new digital feudalism where control equals power.

Transformation of Societal Values

Breaking Traditional Hierarchies

Beyond technology, traditional models of security, wealth, justice, and freedom are evolving. No longer discrete boxes, these values exist as dynamic, interdependent “quantum states” that continuously collapse and reform. As digital transformation and creative destruction reshape infrastructures and societal norms, convenience may mask profound shifts in our technological and cultural metabolism.

  • Mobile = Digital drive‐thru: Fast, scalable, but metabolically disruptive.
  • AI = Industrialization of code: Transition from bespoke craftsmanship to automated abundance.
  • Transformation = Rebalancing societal values: Traditional hierarchies break down.

This seed encapsulates how software’s evolution—from delivery to production—is remolding our digital and societal landscape.

Project Seed: System Instructions & Persona Adoption in AI

Overview

Guiding AI with System Instructions

This seed explores how system instructions enhance AI interactions by providing narrative context and enabling the model to adopt detailed personas. Rather than simply following a script, the AI is given a full backstory, cultural norms, and world history to guide its behavior.

By using system instructions, the AI can generate contextually appropriate responses in complex scenarios—such as a climate survivor narrative—where every detail of the persona shapes its performance.

Key Concepts

System Instructions and Persona Adoption

  • System Instructions: Inject narrative context and speculative details beyond training data.
  • Persona Adoption: Provide comprehensive backstories that guide AI behavior.
  • Narrative Structure: Shape responses by framing scenarios like an actor preparing for a role.

Resources & Usage

Implementation Guidelines

  • Refer to the OpenAI and Google Gemini API documentation for best practices.
  • Test with simple prompts and iterate based on response quality.
  • Focus on both expected and unexpected behaviors and adjust system instructions accordingly.

Digital Media Essays

Essay 1: Identity in Digital Media

Performance & Performativity (Goffman, Auslander, Turkle)

Digital media forces us to rethink identity as an ongoing performance. Erving Goffman argues that identity is managed through careful presentation – a process called "impression management" – where users curate their public personas on platforms like social media.

Philip Auslander extends this idea by emphasizing that digital culture blurs the boundaries between live and recorded performances, making authenticity itself a performance. Sherry Turkle shows that online identities are fluid and multifaceted, continuously reassembled through interactions.

In sum, identity in digital media is dynamic and adaptive—a performance shaped by both human intent and algorithmic feedback.

Essay 2: Film Theory in Digital Media

Benjamin & Bazin Revisited

Film theory remains a powerful tool for understanding digital media. Walter Benjamin observed that mechanical reproduction alters the relationship between art and its audience, a notion intensified by the infinite duplication of digital artifacts.

André Bazin’s emphasis on realism—rooted in the photographic basis of cinema—finds new expression in digital media, where high-definition visuals create immersive experiences even as synthetic elements challenge traditional authenticity.

Together, Benjamin and Bazin help us navigate the tension between reproducibility and immersive realism in the digital age.

Essay 3: Digital Play and Taxonomies

Insights from Huizinga, Caillois, & Sutton-Smith

Digital artifacts transform play into a complex, dynamic process. Huizinga’s Homo Ludens posits play as a cultural foundation of shared rules and symbolic interaction. Digital platforms build communities around game mechanics that echo this idea.

Roger Caillois categorizes play into competition, chance, mimicry, and sensory disruption—a taxonomy that maps directly onto the varied experiences found in online games and interactive media. Brian Sutton-Smith’s emphasis on play’s inherent ambiguity captures how digital play defies rigid categorization.

Combined, these theories reveal that digital play is not a mere replication of traditional games but an evolving arena for social interaction and creative experimentation.

Essay 4: Alternate Perspective on Digital Play

Redefining Play in the Digital Age

Digital media redefines play by merging familiar traditions with radically new interactions. Huizinga’s insights remind us that play is foundational to culture, while Caillois’s taxonomy dissects its varied modes. Sutton-Smith argues that play is indeterminate and continuously evolving.

In digital contexts, structured game rules and emergent, chaotic interactions coexist—creating spaces where play is both a set of established rituals and a dynamic, transformative process.

This alternate view underscores the layered, ever-shifting nature of digital play, where order and chaos intertwine to foster new cultural expressions.
